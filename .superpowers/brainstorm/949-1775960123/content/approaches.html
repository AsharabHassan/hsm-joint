3 Approaches: Content + Visual Overhaul

Each approach covers visuals, SEO, and GEO — they differ in depth and effort

A

Visual Polish + SEO Fix

Effort: Medium | Timeline: 1-2 weeks

Add real photos, doctor headshots, clinic imagery, and medical diagrams to existing sections. Fix missing SEO infrastructure (sitemap, canonicals, schema on all pages). Restructure existing text with proper H2/H3 hierarchy, FAQ schema everywhere, and keyword-optimised meta tags.


Visuals: Real photos in hero sections, doctor headshots replace initials, clinic photos in LocationTrust, stock anatomy diagrams in educational sections

SEO: Sitemap, canonical URLs, full schema markup on every page, Twitter cards, doctor Person schema

GEO: Basic — add "key takeaway" summary boxes that AI engines can extract


Pros

  • Fast to implement
  • Biggest ROI per hour
  • Fixes critical SEO gaps

Cons

  • Content stays mostly the same
  • Minimal GEO optimisation
  • No new content depth
B

Full Content Rewrite + Visual Overhaul

Effort: High | Timeline: 2-4 weeks

Everything in A, PLUS completely rewrite all page content using a SEO+GEO content framework. Every section gets: scannable structure (bullets, tables, callout boxes), entity-rich language Google understands, citation-ready paragraphs AI engines quote, and real imagery throughout.


Visuals: Everything in A + treatment process photo sequences, before/after-style journey visuals, animated stats, condition-specific anatomy diagrams per body area page

SEO: Everything in A + rewritten copy targeting primary + secondary + long-tail keywords per page, internal linking strategy between pages, NLP-optimised entity coverage

GEO: Full — structured "definitive answer" paragraphs, comparison tables AI can parse, research citation formatting, question-answer pairs throughout content (not just FAQ)


Pros

  • Best possible SEO outcome
  • AI engines will cite your content
  • Visually premium
  • Maximises 13 static pages

Cons

  • Significant rewrite effort
  • Needs photo assets ready
  • Longer timeline
C

B + Programmatic Content Expansion

Effort: Very High | Timeline: 3-5 weeks

Everything in B, PLUS expand each body area into individual condition sub-pages (e.g., /knee-pain/osteoarthritis, /knee-pain/meniscus-tear). Creates ~20+ new pages from existing condition data. Each condition gets its own full page with targeted keywords, schema, and imagery.


Visuals: Everything in B + condition-specific hero images and diagrams per sub-page

SEO: Everything in B + 20+ new indexed pages targeting condition-specific keywords

GEO: Everything in B + each condition page becomes a definitive reference AI engines cite for that specific condition


Pros

  • Maximum SEO surface area
  • Each condition ranks independently
  • Massive topical authority

Cons

  • Most effort by far
  • Need images per condition
  • You said static only — this adds pages
  • Diminishing returns vs. B

My Recommendation: B

You said static pages only — so C adds scope you don't want. A leaves content mostly unchanged, which won't move the needle for GEO. B is the sweet spot: every page gets rewritten content that ranks AND gets cited by AI, plus real imagery that makes the site feel like a premium Harley Street clinic, not a text document.
